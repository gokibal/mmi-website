Popularized several Ragas

Mani Iyer was responsible for putting several rare Ragas on the musical map. Ragas such as Charukesi, Vachaspathi, Gowda Malhar, Vijayanagiri, Janaranjani, Kapi Narayani, Umabharanam etc owe their popularity to him. The list is as follows.

1. Charukesi 7. Gowda Malhar 13. Shudhaseemanthini 19. Ranjani
2. Lathangi 8. Mohanakalyani 14. Janaranjani 20. Hamsanandhi
3. Vachaspathi 9. Kapi Narayani 15. Jeyanthasena 21. Jaganmohini
4. Saraswathi 10. Vijayanagiri 16. Bahudari 22. Valaji
5. Saraswathi Manohari 11. ShudhaBangala 17. Umabharanam 23. Nalinakanti
6. Kannada Gowla 12. Ravichandrika 18. Kanada 24. Sarasangi

Often times he used to sing only one Kriti in a raga (Eg: ‘Nijamarmamu’ in Umabharanam , ‘Sarasa Sama’ in Kapi Narayani etc). But such was his genius that even a single Kriti was enough to propel the Raga into mainstream popularity.

Navagraha Kritis

Madurai Mani Iyer’s fondness for the Navagraha Kritis by Muthuswami Dikshithar stemmed from his great devotion to Navagrahas. He believed that the nine Planets had a strong influence over a person’s life and any problems can be overcome through their worship. Mani Iyer made it a routine to sing these Kritis every morning at home. He also sang these Kritis on the concert platform without fail during the final third of his career.

It can be said without an iota of doubt that he was single-handedly responsible for popularizing the Navagraha Kritis of Dikshithar. The Kritis are as follows.


1. Suryamurte, Raga- Sowrashtram
2. Chandram Bhaje, Raga- Asaveri
3. Angarakam Ashrayamyaham, Raga- Surutti
4. Budham Ashrayami, Raga- Natakurunji
5. Brihaspate, Raga- Atana
6. Sri Sukra Bhagavantham, Raga- Paraju
7. Diwakara Thanujam, Raga- Yadukula Kambhoji
8. Smaramyaham, Raga- Ramapriya
9. Mahasuram, Raga- Shanmukhapriya

Tamil songs

Madurai Mani Iyer also played an important role in creating interest in Tamil songs amongst the concert going public. Several of Papanasam Sivan’s songs owe their popularity solely to him. People in his concerts would wait with bated breath for Songs like ‘Kaana kan kodi vendum’ or ‘Paamalai ku inai undo’ to be rendered.

He also popularized Gopalakrishna Bharati’s songs like ‘Sabhapathikku’ and ‘Eppo Varuaro’. The Thukkuda items in his concerts would invariably be comprised of Tamil songs like ‘Kandan karunai’, ‘vellai thamarai’ or ‘paarukulle’.

Madurai Mani Iyer has contributed immensely to the cause of Tamil Isai. He used to sing many Tamil Kritis before the Tamil Isai Movement even gained traction. He had an unbroken stint with the Tamil Isai Sangam from when it was started in 1941, and was one of the earliest recipient of the Isai perarignar (1963) honor from the Sangam.
